Privacy Policy

Overview

TimeDash runs entirely in your browser. It has no analytics, telemetry, ads, or remote tracking. There are no accounts or servers logging your browsing history. Your data stays on your device and is processed using built-in browser storage APIs.

Data Storage

TimeDash saves your preferences and history in Chrome's local storage API (chrome.storage.local). Nothing is written outside your browser profile. The extension stores:

  • Time tracking logs and per-domain history
  • Playback speed preferences per site
  • Site blocking and daily restriction rules
  • Theme and UI customization options

This data is used only to display your usage stats and enforce your rules. No part of it is sent to external servers.

Network Activity

TimeDash makes no outgoing network calls. Any network requests seen in DevTools come from your browser resolving site favicons or loading extension resources locally.

Permissions

TimeDash requests only the permissions needed to track time and enforce your rules:

  1. storage: Saves settings and history locally.
  2. tabs: Reads active tab URLs to record time and apply site rules.
  3. scripting: Injects video speed controls into web video players.
  4. webNavigation: Follows URL navigation across frames.
  5. host_permissions: Lets content scripts run on visited sites to track time and manage video playback.
  6. alarms: Runs daily resets and optional data purges.
  7. notifications: (Optional) Alerts you when daily time limits are reached.

Data Deletion

You own your data. You can delete single domain records or wipe all data using the controls in Data Management above. Uninstalling TimeDash removes all stored extension data from your browser automatically.

  • Click Delete All Data to wipe every record right away.
  • Turn on Auto-purge Old Data to clear older records automatically.
  • Use Delete Specific Data to search and delete records for a single domain.

No Account Required

TimeDash requires no account, email, or cloud sync. All data lives on your device under your control.

Third-Party Code

TimeDash uses open-source libraries for development tooling only. The distributed extension contains no third-party tracking, analytics, or external SDKs.
